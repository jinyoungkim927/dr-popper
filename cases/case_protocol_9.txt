CASE PROTOCOL 9: Case History A 58-year-old man presented to his local GP with a two-hour history of severe chest pain. The pain had commenced while running, and was initially associated with nausea, vomiting and agitation. The patient had experienced similar, less severe chest pain while running over the previous three weeks. He had a 27-year history of type 1 diabetes mellitus.  On examination the man was distressed, diaphoretic and mildly obese. Blood pressure was 165/105 mm Hg, pulse rate 114/min with frequent ventricular ectopic beats (VEBs) and there was an S4 heard on auscultation of the precordium.
