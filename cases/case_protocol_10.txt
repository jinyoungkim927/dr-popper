CASE PROTOCOL 10: Case History An 81-year-old man was brought by ambulance to the Emergency Department with severe back pain. The pain had been intermittent for six days but had become increasingly severe in the past four hours. He had a history of myocardial infarction six years previously and a strong family history of ischaemic heart disease. He was also suffering from hypertension, hypercholesterolaemia and gout. There was no history of gastrointestinal, renal or hepato-biliary disease.  Examination revealed a distressed, pale old man with ankle oedema. There was an expansile, pulsating mass 6 cm in diameter, in the epigastrium. A systolic bruit was audible over the mass as well as over the femoral arteries. There was mild cardiomegaly, an S3 gallop rhythm and his chest was clear.   Questions 1.  What is the most likely cause of this man's abdominal mass?
