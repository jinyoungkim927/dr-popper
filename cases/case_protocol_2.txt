CASE PROTOCOL 2: Case History An obese 56-year-old woman with long-standing type 2 diabetes presented to her gynaecologist with bleeding per vagina, which alarmed her because she had been menopausal for six years. During that time, she had regular Pap smear screening, which had never shown any abnormalities. She had taken hormone replacement therapy (HRT) for several years but had ceased three years ago. There were no other symptoms, and no abnormalities were detected on physical examination. Specifically, the involuted uterus was of normal size, and the cervix was mobile. Questions 1.  What is the definition of post-menopausal bleeding per vagina, and what are the likely causes in this woman?
