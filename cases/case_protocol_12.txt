CASE PROTOCOL 12: Case History A 19-year-old female medical student presented to the Emergency Department with severe bloody diarrhoea. The diarrhoea had commenced three weeks earlier and had gradually worsened, so that each day she was passing 8-10 stools containing stringy mucus and blood. The diarrhoea was associated with crampy lower abdominal pain, urgency and tenesmus.  On examination, she was pale, thin, lethargic and dehydrated. There was tenderness in the left lower quadrant without guarding or rigidity, and the bowel sounds were increased.
